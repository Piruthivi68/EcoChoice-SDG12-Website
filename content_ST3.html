Overview of SDG 12

Goal 12 of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals aims to ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns. This involves:

  • Reducing global food waste by half at retail and consumer levels
  • Achieving sustainable management and efficient use of natural resources
  • Substantially reducing waste generation through prevention, reduction, recycling and reuse
  • Encouraging companies to adopt sustainable practices

Key Challenges

Food Waste

Approximately 1/3 of all food produced globally is lost or wasted, while 800 million people go hungry.

E-Waste

The world generates over 50 million metric tons of electronic waste annually, with only 20% formally recycled.

Fast Fashion

The fashion industry produces 10% of global carbon emissions and is the second-largest consumer of water worldwide.

Sustainable Solutions

Individual Actions

  • Reduce single-use plastics by carrying reusable bags, bottles, and containers
  • Practice mindful shopping - buy only what you need
  • Repair and reuse items instead of replacing them
  • Recycle properly and compost organic waste
  • Choose energy-efficient appliances and products

Business Innovations

Circular economy models that design out waste and keep materials in use

Sustainable packaging solutions using biodegradable materials

Successful Case Studies

Zero Waste Cities

San Francisco has achieved an 80% diversion rate from landfills through comprehensive recycling and composting programs.

Sustainable Fashion Brands

Companies like Patagonia are leading the way with recycled materials and repair programs to extend product lifecycles.

How You Can Get Involved

  • Join local repair cafes and swap events
  • Support businesses with sustainable practices
  • Advocate for better recycling infrastructure in your community
  • Educate others about responsible consumption
  • Participate in our volunteer programs
